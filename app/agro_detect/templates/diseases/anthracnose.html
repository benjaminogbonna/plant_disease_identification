ANTHRACNOSE (Leaf spot) OF SORGHUM

Symptoms

  • The disease appears as small red coloured spots on both surfaces of the leaf
  • The centre of the spot is white in colour encircled by red, purple or brown margin
  • Numerous small black dots like acervuli are seen on the white surface of the lesions
  • The infection deteriorates the physical and chemical quality of the grains and causes a reduction in grain yield of up to 25%
  • The disease is caused by the fungus Colletotrichum sublineola

Prevention
  1. Resistant hybrids may be available information is available from research centres and suppliers
  2. Use certified or clean seed from disease free plants; this is important as this disease can be transmitted by the seeds
  3. Remove other susceptible host plants such as grasses which may harbour the disease
  4. Burn plant residues after harvesting and immediately deep plough the field as the fungus can survive on crop residues
  5. Rotate sorghum on a 2-year basis with crops other than cereals such as chickpeas or beans
  6. In poorly rotated fields, plough under infested residues where soil erosion is not a problem
  7. Thiram can be used as a seed treatment. Thiram 80% WP (Thiram Granuflo 80 WP) can be used as a seed treatment. Apply as a water slurry at a rate of 190-260g to 100kg of seed. Apply only to good quality seed and ensure uniform coverage. Treat seed immediately prior to sowing and do not store seed treated.

Direct control
  • Remove and dispose of infected plants at the earliest stage possible by burning or burying
