PHOSPHORUS DEFICIENCY IN MAIZE

Symptoms

  • Young plant look dwarfed and thin with dark green leaves.
  • It may also manifest by reddish discoloration at juvenile stages of growth
  • Leaf margins veins and stems show purple tints which may spread over the whole leaf blade
  • The disease is caused by phosphorus deficiency in the farm soil.

Prevention
  1. Apply manure e.g. well decomposed compost manure made from plants. Apply 12.5 tonnes/Ha at least five weeks before planting maize. This can be achieved by applying an equivalent of 20 L tin of manure (16-18 Kg) by broadcasting every 8 metres/steps. If manure is not adequate, apply 2 handfuls (0.5-1.5 kg) per planting station and mix with the soil before planting
  2. Apply inorganic fertilizers such as 23:10:5+6S+1.0Zn at the rate of 100 kg/Ha using a bottle top of a 300 ml glass Fanta bottle with lining inside at 10 cm away from the planting station and 10 cm deep with planting stations spaced at 25 cm apart (one-one planting pattern)
  3. Neutralize acidic soils, adequate drainage of crop fields, reduce high iron content in the soil
